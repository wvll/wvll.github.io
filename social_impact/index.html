Social impact of the conference:

WVLL 2026 is poised to create a significant social impact by addressing challenges in applying computer vision to languages with limited resources. The conference's focus on promoting inclusivity and empowering linguistic diversity can bridge the digital divide, ensuring equitable access to technology advancements for speakers of all languages.

Through advancements in document image processing, optical character recognition, and scene text recognition tailored to low-resource languages, the conference can improve access to information and preserve cultural heritage. Digitizing historical texts, medical records, and cultural artifacts can facilitate knowledge dissemination and enrich educational resources in these linguistic communities.

Additionally, vision-based technologies can have a direct impact on healthcare, enhancing medical diagnosis and access to healthcare information. By fostering international collaboration, the conference can lead to shared resources, best practices, and solutions for addressing the unique challenges faced by low-resource languages.

The conference's outcomes can inspire future research and innovation, motivating researchers and students to actively engage in this critical area. By raising awareness about the social impact of a vision-based understanding of low-resource languages, the conference can drive policy discussions and contribute to broader sustainable development goals. By leveraging computer vision technologies to address the needs of underrepresented languages, the conference can create a more inclusive and equitable future, ensuring that advancements in technology benefit speakers of all languages worldwide.
